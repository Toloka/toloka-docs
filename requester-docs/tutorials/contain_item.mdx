# Project 1. Does the photo contain a certain object?

In this [project](/docs/glossary#project), you will ask Tolokers if there are shoes in the photo.

## Create a project `{#project}`

To create a project, open [Toloka for requesters](https://platform.toloka.ai/for-requesters/).

In the interface:

1. Choose a preset:

    1. <TutorialsCreateProjectButton/>

        <TutorialsChoosePresetImage/>

    1. Click **Do it myself**.

    1. Select the **Image classification** preset.

1. <TutorialsAddNameDescription/>

    1. In the **Name for Tolokers** field, enter `Are there shoes in the photo?`.

    1. In the **Description for Tolokers** field, enter `Look at the picture and answer if there are shoes in it.`.

    1. Optionally add a **Private comment**.

1. Edit the [task interface](/docs/glossary#task-interface) in the editor you selected:

    <Tabs tabs={[
      { title: 'Template Builder', value: <ContainItemList1/> },
      { title: 'HTML/CSS/JS editor', value: <ContainItemList2/> },
    ]}/>

1. <TolokaRequesterSourceInstructions/>

    Instructions text:

    ```plaintext
    Look at the picture and determine if there are shoes in the picture.
    If there are, click "Yes".
    If there aren't, click "No".
    For example, there are shoes in the photo, so the correct answer is "Yes".
    ```

    <Note mode="note" title="Note">

    To add an image, click <img src="https://tlkfrontprod.azureedge.net/portal-production/static/uploaded/images/1675425175297/1675425175297_original_svg.svg" alt="" /> and provide a link to the image you want to use as an example.

    </Note>

1. <SaveProject/>

## Create a pool `{#create_pool}`
To create a [pool](/docs/glossary#pool):

1. Open the page of the project titled **Are there shoes in the photo?**.

1. Click **Create new pool** on the project page.

1. <TolokaRequesterPoolType/>

1. <TolokaRequesterPoolName/>

1. <TolokaRequesterPoolDescription/>

1. Click **Create**.

1. <TolokaRequesterSourceAudienceSettings/>

    1. <TutorialsLanguageFilter/>

    1. <TutorialsShockingContent/>

    1. <TutorialsPlatformFilter/>

1. (optional) In the **Speed/quality balance** section, specify the desired quality level. Improving quality may reduce the speed of task completion because the pool will be available to fewer Tolokers. Learn more in the [Speed/quality balance](/docs/guide/adjust) section.

1. <TolokaRequesterSourcePriceSettings/>

    1. In **Price per task suite, $**, set the amount of money to pay per task suite done by one Toloker. For example, `0.01`.

    1. <TolokaRequesterSourceOverlapSettings/>

1. <TutorialsQualityControl/>

    1. Click **Add a quality control rule**.

    1. Find the **Rules** block in the list and choose **Control tasks**.

    1. Set a rule for control tasks: If the **number of responses** to control questions **is greater than or equal to 3** and the **percentage of correct responses** to control questions **is less than 60**, then **restrict the Toloker's access** to the **project for 10 days**. Specify the **Control task** as a reason.

        <Image width="700px" withBackground imageId="1675426046741" alt="" />

        Learn more in [Quality control](/docs/guide/control).

1. <TutorialsTimePerTaskSuite/>

    The time should be long enough to read the instructions and wait for task data to load. For example, `600` seconds.

1. <TutorialsUploadTasks/>

    1. Prepare your own task file. Check out the example in [the demo file](https://download.cdn.yandex.net/from/yandex.ru/support/ru/toloka-requester/files/dataset-shoes.tsv). In the Toloka interface under the pool name, there are links to **file** templates for regular, control, and training tasks.

    1. Click **Drop file here or select**, and upload the file you’ve just made.

    1. Click **Continue**.

    1. Choose **Smart mixing**.

    1. In the **General tasks** field, specify `9`.

    1. In the **Training tasks** field, specify `0`.

    1. In the **Control tasks** field, specify `1`.

    1. Click **Combine tasks into suites**.

1. <TolokaRequesterSourceAddControlTasks/>

    1. In the left column, turn on the **result** option.

    1. Choose the correct answer to the question.

    1. Click **Save and go to next**.

    1. Click **Are there shoes in the photo?** to exit the [task markup](/docs/glossary#task-markup) mode.

        <Note mode="note" title="Note">

        In small pools, [control tasks](/docs/glossary#control-task) should be 10-20% of all tasks. Include different versions of correct responses in equal amounts. See the distribution of responses on the **Edit tasks** page, **Control tasks** tab.

        </Note>

1. <TutorialsDoubleCheck/>

    <TolokaRequesterSourceStepEnabled/>

1. Click <img src="https://tlkfrontprod.azureedge.net/portal-production/static/uploaded/images/1675425184616/1675425184616_original_svg.svg" alt="" /> to start the pool.

## Get the results `{#get_results}`
To get the results:

1. Next to the **Download results** button, click <img src="https://tlkfrontprod.azureedge.net/portal-production/static/uploaded/images/1675425179291/1675425179291_original_svg.svg" alt="Drop-down button" />.

1. Choose the **Dawid-Skene aggregation model**. Learn more about [Result aggregation based on the Dawid-Skene model](/docs/guide/result-aggregation).

1. In the window that opens, click **Yes**.

1. At the top of the page, click **View operations list**.

    <Note mode="note" title="Note">

    To track the progress, refresh the page from time to time. Aggregation takes from 5 to 20 minutes. You can start designing another project meanwhile.

    </Note>

1. When the operation is complete, download the file with the results. To do this, click **Download** in the **Files** column.

1. Use the file with the results in [project 2](/docs/tutorials/find_an_item_in_store).
